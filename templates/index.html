Frequently Asked Questions

Our Purpose!

To educate beginner investors in the importance and less-frightening aspects of investing and to prepare for a better financial future.

What is an Exchanged-Traded Fund?

An Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) is a type of security that involves a collection of securities—such as stocks—that often tracks an underlying index, although they can invest in any number of industry sectors or use various strategies.

What does average annualized return of the portfolio mean?

Our average annualized return represents the expected growth of your investment when you invest your money in the portfolio. If you put in $100 in a portfolio with an average annualized return of 10%, your investment is expected to grow to $110 ($100 + 100 * 10%) by the end of the year.

What is the risk-return tradeoff?

Goal: The goal is to maximize your annual return and minimize your risk. By maximizing your return and minimizing your risk, you are aiming for the highest amount of money you can earn with the lowest chance of losing it….. (Needs rephrasing)

Why Invest?

A common question for people is typically, “Why should we invest?” Investing is to provide a life that we want without financial burdens, to prepare us for retirement, and to be ready for unexpected emergencies. From survey conducted by Blackrock, it shows that 51% participants responded that they are not making enough money to live the life they want; 43% responded with not having money to take care of unexpected emergencies, and 38% participants are not on track for retirement. Another research by Yahoo Finance shows that 64% of Americans are unprepared for retirement. Developing better financial habits prepare us for a sustainable future.

Why invest in a portfolio instead of individualized stocks?

As novice investors, the easiest way to invest is to start with a portfolio that represents a diversified range of assets (stocks, government bonds, Treasury Bills, etc.). By investing in a portfolio, the overall risk is minimized because we are not putting all our investment into one company, but instead many companies. If some companies perform well, and some perform poorly, the chance of losing all our investment is mitigated.

Are there downsides to investing in portfolios?

In most instances, financial asset management companies charge a fee for their services (either by commission or a percentage of your earnings from investments). However, in the long run, such as retirement, the benefits outweigh the cost because asset management companies have better understanding of the market and know where to better invest your money to make sure you have the best earnings based on your risk tolerance. What are asset classes and how do they work?

When you invest in a portfolio, it consists of many different asset classes, which consists of either stock, bonds, Treasury Bills. Etc. With each asset, you are putting in your money, in hopes of that money generates more money for you. Refer to the link for a more in-depth description of each type of asset classes, Investopedia - Asset Classes

What if I do not have much money to start investing?

It is perfectly okay even if you do not have much money to start investing. As long as you can put a little amount in each month, the money you put in accumulates interest, and over time, your money will grow more.
